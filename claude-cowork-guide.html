What is Claude Cowork?

The short version: it's Claude doing the heavy lifting, not just answering questions.

"Most AI tools are built around the prompt. Claude Cowork is built around the outcome."
— Anthropic. Instead of going back and forth, you hand Cowork a goal and come back to a finished deliverable.
🤖

Agentic, not just chatty

Cowork can autonomously take multi-step actions — opening files, running subtasks, and producing polished outputs — without you steering every move.

💻

Lives on your desktop

It runs locally via the Claude Desktop app, with direct access to your files and folders — no manual uploads needed.

🔒

Runs in a secure sandbox

Cowork operates in an isolated virtual machine on your computer, keeping file and network access controlled and safe.

⏱

No timeout stress

Unlike regular chat, Cowork handles long-running tasks without sessions timing out mid-way through your work.

📦

Professional outputs

Think actual Excel files with formulas, formatted PowerPoints, structured Word docs — not just text to copy-paste.

🧪

Research preview

Cowork is actively evolving. Available on paid plans (Pro, Max, Team, Enterprise) via the Claude Desktop app.

Getting Set Up

You're three steps away from your first Cowork task.

1

Download Claude Desktop

Head to claude.ai/download and grab the app for macOS (11+) or Windows 10+. Install and sign in with your Anthropic account.

2

Make sure you're on a paid plan

Cowork requires Pro, Max, Team, or Enterprise. Free accounts don't have access yet. Check your billing settings to confirm.

3

Switch to the Cowork tab

In the Claude Desktop app, you'll see a "Cowork" tab next to "Chat". Click it, describe your task, review Claude's plan, and let it go.

Projects

Projects give you a dedicated local folder with its own context files, working files, scheduled tasks, and conversation history. Think of it like having separate desks for separate jobs — your memory and files don't bleed between them. Create one per client, project, or workflow.

Giving Claude Context

Cowork out of the box is fine. Cowork with good context is genuinely impressive.

Pro tip from the community: The gap between a mediocre Cowork setup and an excellent one is about 30 minutes of upfront configuration. Instead of writing longer prompts, give Claude better source material.

⚙️ Global Instructions

  • Go to Settings → Cowork in the desktop app
  • Write your preferred tone, output format, and working style
  • These apply across all Cowork tasks by default
  • Example: "Always write in a concise, plain-English style. Avoid jargon."

📂 Folder / Project Instructions

  • Add instructions specific to a project folder or workspace
  • Useful when different projects need different styles or rules
  • Example: one project may be client-facing (formal), another internal (casual)
  • Set these when selecting a local folder in a Project

Context Files

The single biggest upgrade you can make to your Cowork setup.

1

Create a context folder

Make a folder (e.g. _context/) in your project. This is where you'll store background info for Claude to reference.

2

Add relevant background files

Think: a company overview, your role description, terminology glossary, example outputs you like, key contacts, brand voice guide, recurring report templates.

3

Reference them in your task

When starting a task, point Claude to your context folder. It'll read the files and use them to calibrate its output to your specific needs.

What Makes a Good Task Description?

✅ Include these things

  • What the finished output should look like
  • The audience (internal team? client? exec?)
  • The format you want (Excel, Word, PDF, Markdown...)
  • Any specific constraints or must-haves
  • Where to find the source files

❌ Avoid these pitfalls

  • Being vague about the desired outcome
  • Forgetting to specify the output format
  • Assuming Claude knows your company's jargon
  • Giving one huge multi-objective task (break it up!)
  • Skipping the review step before Claude executes

Not sure how to structure a task?

Jump into Claude and ask it to help you write the brief — meta, but it works brilliantly.

Plugins & Skills

The quickest way to level up Cowork for your specific kind of work.

Plugins bundle together skills, connectors, and sub-agents into one ready-made package — so you don't have to set everything up from scratch.

Installing Plugins

1

Open the Cowork tab in Claude Desktop

Plugins live inside Cowork, not in the regular Chat tab.

2

Click "Customize" in the left sidebar

This opens the customisation panel where plugins, skills, and connectors live.

3

Browse and install

Click "Browse plugins" to see what's available. Hit Install on anything relevant. Plugins are saved locally on your machine.

4

Use skills with "/" or "+"

Once installed, access plugin skills inside a conversation by typing / or clicking the + button to see what's available.

Sub-agents: Parallel Power

⚡

Work in parallel, not in sequence

Sub-agents let Cowork split work across multiple Claude instances at once. Processing 10 files one-by-one might take ~30 minutes. Running them in parallel via sub-agents: around 4 minutes. Look for plugins that include sub-agent support for batch processing tasks.

Tips & Tricks

Stuff the community has figured out — so you don't have to.

Projects give each area of your work its own memory, files, and scheduled tasks. Memory doesn't carry over between standalone sessions — only within Projects. If you want Claude to remember context between sessions, always work inside a Project.
Tested by the community: one giant task with many objectives produces noticeably worse results than splitting it into focused, smaller tasks. Instead of "do everything for my monthly report", break it into: "extract figures from these 5 files", then "synthesise into a summary", then "format as a Word doc". Same logic applies to skills — multiple focused skills beat one mega-skill.
Scheduled tasks are one of the most underused Cowork features. Describe a recurring job once — weekly team summary, daily inbox triage, monthly report generation — and Cowork runs it automatically. Find this under the Cowork tab in the app.
Before Cowork starts working, it shows you its approach. Don't skip this step! This is your chance to catch misunderstandings, redirect the scope, or add constraints you forgot to mention. A 30-second review here can save you a 10-minute redo.
Dispatch lets you start and monitor Cowork tasks from your phone. Scan the QR code in the desktop app to pair your Claude mobile app. Your desktop then does the heavy lifting while you're away from your desk — handy for long tasks you want to fire off during a commute.
More context isn't always better. A focused 2-page company brief will outperform a sprawling 40-page doc where the key info is buried. Keep context files concise, clearly structured, and specific to what Claude actually needs for that project.
Cowork has 38+ connectors for popular apps like Notion, Slack, Google Drive, Asana, and more. Connecting these means Claude can pull live data (rather than relying on uploaded files) and can take actions in those tools on your behalf — like creating Notion pages or sending Slack messages.
Keep in mind: the desktop app must stay open during tasks. Sessions aren't shareable with others. And Cowork isn't currently suitable for regulated workloads that require audit logs (e.g. certain compliance or legal contexts). For those use cases, check with your IT or compliance team first.
